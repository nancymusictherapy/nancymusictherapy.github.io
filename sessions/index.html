What Does the Process Look Like?

  1. Music therapy can begin with a referral from another healthcare provider or an educator. Individuals may also refer themselves to a music therapist.

  2. To identify the clientâ€™s needs and to establish goals, the therapist completes an initial assessment through interview, records review, and by observing their response to strategically-chosen music therapy techniques.

  3. The therapist develops music therapy interventions and carries out a treatment plan with the client. The plan is reassessed with each session and adjusted as required to help the client meet their identified goals.

  4. When goals are met by the client, the therapist gradually fades music cues to help facilitate generalization to non-music settings.
