What Are Music Therapy Interventions?

Music therapy is comprised of two equally important parts, the client-therapist relationship and the interventions, which are essentially interactive therapeutic activities. Interventions are adapted to suit the clientâ€™s cognitive and physical functioning abilities. While certain interventions are scientifically-proven to be effective within a specific clinical population, they are often used as a starting point rather than a prescription for treatment.

Common music therapy interventions include:

  • Drumming
  • Vocal and/or Instrumental improvisation
  • Life review/reminiscence
  • Song-writing and/or lyric analysis
  • Melodic Intonation Therapy
  • Movement to music
  • Music and imagery
  • Relaxation/stress reduction
  • Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation
  • Sensory integration
  • Singing and/or toning
  • Vibrotactile stimulation
